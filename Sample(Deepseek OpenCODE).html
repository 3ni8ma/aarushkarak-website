About

Redefining access to space

Founded by engineers from the world's leading aerospace organizations, Aerus is on a mission to make space travel routine and reliable.

We design, manufacture, and operate advanced spacecraft for commercial, scientific, and government missions. Our vertically integrated approach allows us to iterate rapidly and deliver unmatched performance at a fraction of traditional costs.

From satellite deployment to crewed missions, every Aerus vehicle is built with precision engineering, modern avionics, and a relentless focus on safety.

Mission

Our road ahead

We are scaling rapidly toward a future where space is accessible to all. Here's what we've achieved and where we're headed.

Every milestone brings us closer to our vision of making humanity multi-planetary. We're investing heavily in next-generation propulsion, orbital refueling, and advanced manufacturing to reduce cost and increase flight rate.

Our roadmap includes lunar cargo missions, a crewed orbital tourism program, and the first stage of a permanent low-Earth orbit platform.

2023

First orbital launch

Aerus Nova completes its maiden flight, delivering 12 satellites to orbit.

2025

First stage recovery

Successful landing of Nova first stage on drone ship — reusability proven.

2027

Crewed flight

Halo spacecraft carries first Aerus astronauts to the International Space Station.

2028+

Lunar & beyond

Uncrewed lunar cargo delivery and development of next-gen deep-space vehicle.
